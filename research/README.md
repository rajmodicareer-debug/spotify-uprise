Case Study: Spotify Uprise
Emerging artists face major roadblocks in getting noticed, improving their music, and building a loyal audience. While Spotify provides a platform for music distribution and offers some analytics via Spotify for Artists, it lacks tools that enable:
• Early-stage feedback loops before tracks go public
• Audience interaction at a granular, timestamped level
• Creative transparency and behind-the-scenes storytelling
• AI-driven coaching to help artists refine their sound
• Community engagement through short-form music content

These limitations prevent rising musicians from truly learning, iterating, and growing on the platform. The existing ecosystem is focused on already-released music, with little to no support for artists who are still developing their tracks or fanbase.
What Spotify Stages Adds (That Spotify Currently Doesn’t Have)
Spotify Stages introduces a brand-new layer within the Spotify ecosystem focused entirely on raw, in-progress, or unreleased tracks. It empowers emerging artists with features like:
• Pre-release uploads with options to share inspiration, creative process, and progress updates
• Behind-the-scenes storytelling via text or voice notes
• Timestamped listener feedback that helps artists pinpoint what’s working and what’s not.
• AI-powered coaching that delivers precision feedback based on listener engagement and audio analysis
• Ripple Score – a unique metric that reflects how a track emotionally or virally impacted Listeners
• Short-form music reels to increase discoverability
• Fan-guided iteration, where the community helps shape the final version of a trackThis functionality transforms Spotify from a music consumption platform into a music co-creation and growth platform, addressing critical gaps for emerging talent.
